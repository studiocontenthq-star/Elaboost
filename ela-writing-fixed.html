Grade 5 → Grade 6 Writing Bridge

Master these Grade 5 skills now — they're the foundation for everything in middle school. The same rubric categories apply all the way through!

📋 Georgia Milestone Writing Rubric
All writing is scored on these 4 dimensions. Each is worth 1–4 points. Know these = know how to get a better score.
💡 Ideas & Content
Is your main idea clear? Do you have specific details, examples, or evidence? Does everything connect to your topic?
4 = Strong, specific details throughout
3 = Mostly clear with some support
2 = Main idea present but thin
1 = Unclear or off-topic
🗂️ Organization
Does your writing have a clear beginning, middle, and end? Do you use transitions? Does it flow logically?
4 = Strong intro, body, conclusion + transitions
3 = Mostly organized, minor gaps
2 = Some structure but choppy
1 = Hard to follow
🎨 Style & Voice
Does your writing sound like you? Do you use interesting word choices? Is the tone right for your audience?
4 = Engaging, varied sentences, strong vocab
3 = Some personality, decent word choice
2 = Flat or repetitive
1 = Very basic
✏️ Conventions
Is your spelling mostly correct? Do you use punctuation and capitalization properly? Are your sentences complete?
4 = Very few errors, easy to read
3 = Some errors but readable
2 = Frequent errors, distracting
1 = Hard to read
🛠️ Writing Tips by Type
Quick reminders for each essay type on the Georgia Milestone.

✊ Opinion (W.1)

  • Start: "I believe that..."
  • Use: because, therefore, for this reason
  • Each body paragraph = 1 reason + evidence
  • End: restate opinion differently
  • Never say "I think I think"!

📘 Informational (W.2)

  • Hook → topic sentence → facts
  • Use: first, next, also, in addition
  • Define tricky words
  • No opinions — facts only!
  • End with a "so what" sentence

📖 Narrative (W.3)

  • Hook first sentence!
  • Show, don't tell feelings
  • Add dialogue with quotes
  • Use: suddenly, after a while, finally
  • Ending = reflection or change
🚀 Grade 6 Preview — What's Coming
These are the same skills, leveled up for middle school.

📝 Argument Writing

  • Same as opinion but more formal
  • Must acknowledge opposing view
  • Cite sources by name
  • Stronger vocabulary expected

🔬 Research Writing

  • Uses multiple sources
  • Must summarize AND paraphrase
  • Longer (4–5 paragraphs)
  • Works Cited required

📚 Literary Analysis

  • Write about books/stories
  • Use text evidence with quotes
  • Analyze theme + character
  • Academic tone required
